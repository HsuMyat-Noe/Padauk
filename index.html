Overview

The Padauk flower, scientifically known as Pterocarpus indicus, is a vibrant and beautiful flower native to Southeast Asia. It blooms in bright yellow clusters and is cherished for its beauty and cultural significance.

Characteristics

  • Bright yellow
  • Typically during the rainy season
  • Mildly fragrant
  • Small, clustered blooms

Cultural Significance

The Padauk flower holds a special place in the hearts of the people of Myanmar. It is often associated with strength and durability, much like the wood of the Padauk tree.

During the Burmese New Year, known as Thingyan, the Padauk flower is prominently featured in celebrations.Myanmar people give Padauk flower to their beloved person.

The first month of the Burmese traditional lunar calendar starts from mid-April, when Padauk flowers bloom. So, it is closely associated with New Year Festival.
